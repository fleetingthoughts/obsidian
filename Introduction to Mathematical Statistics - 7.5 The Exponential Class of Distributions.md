From the [7.3](Introduction%20to%20Mathematical%20Statistics%20-%207.3%20Properties%20of%20a%20%20Sufficient%20Statistic.md) and [7.4](Introduction%20to%20Mathematical%20Statistics%20-%207.4%20Completeness%20and%20Uniqueness.md), we are motivated to find complete and sufficient statistics because unbiased function of a complete and sufficient statistic is the MVUE of the unknown parameter. One approach is to find a class of functions that are complete and see if any sufficient statistics have these class of functions as a pdf. One such function is the regular exponential class that includes the normal distribution. We apply our results to determine complete and sufficient statistics for random variables distributed in an exponential class of functions:
1) Definition of the regular exponential class and the conditions required
2) The definition of the regular exponential makes it easy to determine one form of the sufficient statistic
3) The mean, variance, and the pdf of the statistic
4) The canonical statistic is sufficient and complete
