Derive the integral for a general closed curve that is invariant to the coordinate system or parametric representation of the curve.
1) Demonstrate properties of our the basic formula for the oriented area $A$ of a curve $C$ before demonstrating that it represents the area of the closed curve:
	1) The equivalence of the 3 different formulations of the line integral area. The proof outline follows from integration by parts.
	2) The formula from 1) is invariant to rigid transformations.
	3) The additive properties of the integral allow us to compute the area with a reversed orientation or a closed curve constructed from a sum of other curves
2) Geometric appeal to demonstrate that the basic formula calculates the area enclosed by two vertical lines and two curves. Another way to see it is that the formula established in 1) is to satisfy this geometric understanding
	- The convention chosen is that the clockwise orientation results in a positive area. This is where the positive side of the curve comes from. The counterclockwise convention gives us an easier formula for the polar coordinates.
3) The basic formula expressed in polar coordinates derived from the definition of the basic formula in cartesian coordinates
4) Alternative derivation of the formula for the area in polar coordinates from the definition of integrals
