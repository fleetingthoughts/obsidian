If $X$ is a random variable with a Poisson distribution, therefore it has a parameter $\lambda$ and the support is $x = n \in \mathbb{N}$ the probability of $X= n$ \can be calculated from the probability of $X=n-1$, that is because the Poisson distribution exhibits a recursive property that can be proved by induction: $$P(X = n) = \frac{\lambda}{n}P(n-1)$$
