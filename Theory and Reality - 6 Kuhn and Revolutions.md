Now discussing Revolutions, as described in [Theory and Reality - 5 Kuhn and Normal Science](Theory%20and%20Reality%20-%205%20Kuhn%20and%20Normal%20Science.md), the breakdown of normal science and subsequent revolutions are triggered by two things:
1) A critical amount of anomalies resulting in a crisis
2) A competing paradigm to take its place characterized by its ability to problem-solve the anomalies (more on this problem-solving metric later)
Its worth contrasting this with Popper who would've deemed that 1) was sufficient to overthrow a paradigm. It is also worth noting that the problem-solving capabilities of the new paradigm could not be described by a philosophical theory of evidence or testing and that they are instead more subjective. Kuhn softens this position in his later work by stating that there is a common "shared basis for theory selection (p.90-91)" such as predictive power, be able to explain observations, etc... but this alone is not enough to characterize a paradigm as there is no such universal formula. Instead, the paradigm is characterized by its devaluing and emphasizing of the common basis shared by paradigms. Some paradigms will value quantitative prediction over qualitative prediction for example. PGS's interpretation is that Kuhn believes this disorder and scientific revolution is good for Science.

PGS also counter Kuhn's description here in his own field of Biology quoting that it is very hard to find instances of these "crisis" with a critical amount of anomalies in biology so perhaps 1) is not needed at all and believes that Kuhn was motivated by a need for a hypothetical mechanism to incentivize people to move to a paradigm's competition.

We further characterize the competing paradigms and what distinguishes two paradigms and its larger implications in the ideas of relativism and what it means for scientific progress. Kuhn has stated that during peaceful "Normal Science", only one paradigm can exist at a time because paradigms are incommensurable with each other and would lead to disorganization preventing Normal Science from happening in the first place (See PGS's anecdote on AI life in last chapter). This incommensurability was described by Kuhn in 2 parts:
1) Linguistic incommensurability: a literal language barrier between scientists belong to 2 different paradigms that use terms differently resulting in miscommunication inconducive to normal science.

- Revolutionary change distinguished from Normal science change by problem solving asopposed to puzzle solving
- Incommensurability of paradigms. Only one paradigms exists at a time. There is a normative reason: it organizes work better
	- linguistic incommensurability cannot communicate effectively with each other. Some complicated stuff about holism
		- PGS argues that no historical examples occur of this miscommunication
	- standard incommensurability different standards in evidence. This is related to a larger field called relativism that tries to explain the basis for the difference in standards
		- Paradigm determiens what kind of theory: causal explanation vs abstract (i.e. mathematical )formulation. See newton for mathematical 
- Scientific progress:
	- Subjective view of progress between paradigms. History written by victor. This is a relativist vew. (no such thing as objective truth. All truth is relative)
	- Objective view of progress: Paradigms improve in problem solving efficiency.
		- PGS criticizes this as this gives a common baseline between paradigms and conflicts with his incommensurability of paradigms. How can we decide one paradigm has better problem solving if we can't agree on which problems are important
Closing thoughts
- Logical positivists and Popper viewed scientific practices in terms of logic/language epistomlological theories and that they differed in degree and criteria from normal every day problemsolving. Kuhn introduced the idea that it is a social organization.
- Introduced the idea of science being thought of at the organizational vs individual level. Like the invisible hand of the free market formed by several self-interested individuals, the invisible hand of science Science is a sum greater than its parts moved by
