## Q1 (The Republic, Book 1)

- The Answer: A true _techne_ inherently seeks and provides what is advantageous to its subject matter (the weaker party), not to the practitioner (the stronger party) (ayer-langu... pp. 5, 42).
- Book Section: _The Republic_, Book 1, 341c–342e.
- Context: Socrates is cross-examining Thrasymachus. He shows that a physician, qua physician, treats patients to heal them, and a captain rules sailors to navigate safely.
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Functional Definition. It forces you to look past the superficial corruption or selfishness of individual practitioners to isolate the objective, mathematical definition of a craft (_techne_) as an unselfish domain of expertise.

## Q2 (Language, Truth and Logic)

- The Answer: It has no truth-value (it is neither true nor false) (ayer-langu... p. 7). Its semantic status is literally senseless—it is a purely emotional expression or a masked command (ayer-langu... pp. 7, 9).
- Book Section: Chapter 6: _Critique of Ethics and Theology_, pp. 104–113 (ayer-langu... p. 7).
- Context: Ayer is applying the verification principle to moral philosophy. He argues that saying "Stealing is wrong" adds no factual statement to "You stole that money," but merely expresses moral disapproval (ayer-langu... p. 7).
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Emotivistic Semantic Demarcation. It shows that under a strict verificationist framework, ethical language does not describe world features but reflects psychological states.

## Q3 (The Apology)

- The Answer: (1) Meletus claims Socrates _alone_ corrupts the youth, while everyone else improves them. (2) Socrates shows that in horse training, only a few experts improve horses, while the crowd corrupts them. (3) Meletus claims Socrates corrupts intentionally. (4) Socrates notes that intentional corruption means intentionally harming his own community, which would cause harm to turn back on himself. No sane person wishes to be harmed. Ergo, if he corrupts, it is unintentional, which legally requires education, not a trial.
- Book Section: _The Apology_, 24d–26a.
- Context: Socrates' cross-examination of Meletus during his capital trial.
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Reductio ad Absurdum Implementation. It tracks how to structurally force an opponent’s sweeping moral accusations into impossible psychological contradictions.

## Q4 (Philosophy of Natural Science)

- The Answer: If $H$ implies $I$, and $I$ is true, concluding $H$ is true commits the formal fallacy of affirming the consequent (hempel-phi... p. 13). The schema is deductively invalid because completely different, false hypotheses can easily predict the exact same true outcome $I$ (hempel-phi... p. 13).
- Book Section: Chapter 2: _Scientific Inquiry: Invention and Test_, section 2.2, pp. 197–198 (hempel-phi... pp. 5, 13).
- Context: Hempel evaluates Semmelweis' successful handwashing experiment (hempel-phi... p. 11). Handwashing cut deaths, but Semmelweis' initial hypothesis (that _only_ cadaveric matter causes the fever) was still false, as putrid living matter also caused it (hempel-phi... pp. 11, 13).
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach the Asymmetry of Scientific Verification. It visually fixes the rule that empirical success can establish partial confirmation but never absolute logical truth (hempel-phi... p. 14).

## Q5 (Inter-textual Integration)

- The Answer: They are essentially identical in intent but arrive via different philosophical tracks (ayer-langu... p. 9, hempel-phi... p. 36). Ayer labels any non-tautological, non-empirical statement "metaphysical nonsense" because it fails linguistic meaning criteria (ayer-langu... p. 9). Hempel labels them "pseudo-hypotheses" because they fail the logical machinery of predictive derivation (hempel-phi... p. 36).
- Book Section: Ayer Chapter 1 (ayer-langu... p. 5) / Hempel Chapter 3, section 3.5 (hempel-phi... p. 5).
- Context: Synthesis of Logical Positivism's linguistic focus with Logical Empiricism's focus on scientific testing methodology.
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Synthesized Conceptual Equivalence. It prevents you from memorizing jargon by verifying you understand how different thinkers use separate terms to describe identical logical failures.

- The Answer: Virtue (_arete_) makes the object it is applied to _better_ with respect to that virtue's nature. Harming someone makes them _worse_ with respect to human virtue (justice). Since justice cannot produce injustice, a just person using their justice can never make someone worse (harm them).
- Book Section: _The Republic_, Book 1, 335a–336a.
- Context: Socrates is systematically dismantling Polemarchus' conventional view of justice (ayer-langu... p. 5).
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Elenctic Logic Tracking. It shows how Socrates links the definitions of "virtue," "function," and "harm" to render the concept of "just violence" structurally incoherent.

## Q3 (Language, Truth and Logic)

- The Answer: Ayer shows that the statement _Cogito_ ("there is a thought now") is only logically certain because it cannot negate itself without contradiction (ayer-langu... p. 31). However, "I exist" does not follow from this, because a single transient thought occurring at an isolated moment does not entail a continuous, substantive entity called an "I" that persists across time (ayer-langu... p. 31).
- Book Section: Chapter 2: _The Function of Philosophy_, pp. 30–31 (ayer-langu... pp. 5, 30).
- Context: Ayer targets rationalism's historic attempt to build a deductive picture of the universe from absolute internal "first principles." (ayer-langu... pp. 30-31)
- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach the Deconstruction of Cartesian Foundationalism. It trains you to detect when a philosopher quietly slips a complex, unproven entity (the unified self) into a basic, immediate observation (ayer-langu... p. 31).

## Q4 (The Republic, Book 2–4)

- The Answer: The thesis is that justice is an unnatural, artificial social compromise (ayer-langu... p. 7). By nature, doing injustice is a good, but suffering injustice is an evil that far outweighs the good of doing it. ## Q4 (The Apology)

## Q5 (Philosophy of Natural Science)

- The Answer:  
    $$\text{If } (H \land A) \implies I$$  
    $$\neg I$$  
    $$\therefore \neg(H \land A) \iff (\neg H \lor \neg A)$$
- Book Section: Chapter 3: _The Test of a Hypothesis: Its Logic and Its Force_, section 3.2, p. 213 (hempel-phi... pp. 5, 29).

- Pedagogical Objective & Point: To teach Deductive Vulnerability Mapping. It mathematically exposes why an isolated negative experiment can never act as a magic bullet to kill a theory, since a scientist can always redirect the falsification arrow toward an auxiliary assumption (hempel-phi... p. 29).
