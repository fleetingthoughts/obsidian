Throughout all of Chapter 2, we found incredible results about infinite series by deriving results about simpler sequences and then reducing the question about infinite series to a question about the sequences (i.e. the sequence of its partial sums). We shall do something analogous for the entirety of chapter 6 for functions and say profound things about an infinite series of functions by starting with simpler sequences of functions. We start by asking what we need to say something about the continuity of a function:
- Defining two properties about sequences of functions: point-wise convergence and uniform convergence of functions.
- Uniform convergence AND continuity of the individual terms required for a sequence of functions to converge to a limiting function that is continuous
- Alternative formulations of continuity of limiting functions by Cauchy's method
