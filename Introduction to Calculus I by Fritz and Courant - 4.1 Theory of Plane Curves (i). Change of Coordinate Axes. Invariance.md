Curvature and $\sqrt{\dot{x}^2+\dot{y}^2}$ are invariant under transformations involving rotation and translation:
1) Analytical definition of transformation under rotation and translation. It is not explicitly called this in the book, but the transformation defined is known as a rigid transformation and preserves Euclidean distances between points
2) The rigid transformation from $(x,y)$ to $(\xi, \eta)$ preserves a certain quantity $\sqrt{\dot{x}^2+\dot{y}^2}=\sqrt{\dot{\eta}^2+\dot{\xi}^2}$  and therefore it preserves the velocity
3) Curvature is preserved under rigid transformations.
