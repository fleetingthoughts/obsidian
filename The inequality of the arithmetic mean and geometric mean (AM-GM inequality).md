From the fact that the square of any real number is greater than 0, we can then derive the fact that the geometric mean is strictly less than the arithmetic mean. The fact that $(x-y)^2 > 0$ for any two positive real number $x,y \in \mathbb{R}$ allows us to derive the following:$$\sqrt{xy}\lt \frac{x+y}{2}$$
The only case when they equal is when $x=y$. 

The AM-GM inequality is a fundamental relation used to produce the [algorthm that computes a quantity known as the arithmetic-geometric mean](Algorithm%20compute%20the%20arithmetic%20geometric%20mean%20(AGM)%20of%20any%20two%20positive%20numbers.md) that was popularized by Gauss
