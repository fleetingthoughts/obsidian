Zibaldone is the Italian vernacular for a notebook. While the literal translation holds no value, what is worth noting is the use and the pedagogical applications in the way it was used. The Zibaldone was used for music exercises and the jotting down of any ideas that I find analagous to the fleeting notes in [Zettalkasten](Ahren%20Chapter%201-3%20Intro%20to%20Zettalskan.md). John Mortensen stresses the pedagogical value of the simple act of "ink to paper" and the writing down of ideas as opposed to a more convenient digital "quick and easy" note taking. I see this as a form of active learning that is critical to making an idea of your own. This form of active learning is the essence of the Zibaldone in music taking
