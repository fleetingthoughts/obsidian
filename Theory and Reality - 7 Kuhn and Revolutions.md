- Revolutions is a breakdown of paradigm
	-  How do revolutions occur
- How do they occur:
	- competing paradigm
	- crisis
- PGS counters the necessity for crisis.
- Kuhn elaborates that there exists a shared basis between paradigms on theory selection (predictive power, induction, etc..) but what defines a paradigm is the downplaying and emphasis of some aspects of it
- Revolutionary change distinguished from Normal science change by problem solving asopposed to puzzle solving
- Incommensurability of paradigms. Only one paradigms exists at a time. There is a normative reason: it organizes work better
	- linguistic incommensurability cannot communicate effectively with each other. Some complicated stuff about holism
		- PGS argues that no historical examples occur of this miscommunication
	- standard incommensurability different standards in evidence. This is related to a larger field called relativism that tries to explain the basis for the difference in standards
		- Paradigm determiens what kind of theory: causal explanation vs abstract (i.e. mathematical )formulation. See newton for mathematical 
- Scientific progress:
	- Subjective view of progress between paradigms. History written by victor. This is a relativist vew. (no such thing as objective truth. All truth is relative)
	- Objective view of progress: Paradigms improve in problem solving efficiency.
		- PGS criticizes this as this gives a common baseline between paradigms and conflicts with his incommensurability of paradigms. How can we decide one paradigm has better problem solving if we can't agree on which problems are important
