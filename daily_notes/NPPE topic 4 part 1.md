# Law for Professional Practice
- Provincial vs Federal
	- Can only appeal upward
	- supreme court is highest court of appeal
- Common Law vs Statute law vs case law
	- Statute law is actual law established by legislation
	- common law similar to case law is based on precendent
	- Common law not used in quebec except in federal
		- Operates on civil code and codified statues
- Claims and disputes
	- common in construction
# Types of law
- private vs public
	- public affect societal institutions
	- private is between individuals
- criminal vs civil
	- civil involves compensation
	- criminal law involves punishment
# elements of contracts
1) offer and acceptance
2) intent to accept
3) consideration: both parties benefit 
4) within mental capacity to accept
5) legal purpose: must be legal

- waiver and estoppel
	- waive a legal right
	- estoppel: cannot reinforce something on principle that was previously waived
- quantum meruit: value for work done. Compnensation for unseen circumstances.
- breach
	- inadvertence
- when breach occurs
	- injunction (prohibitory): halt something until compliance is achieved
	- specific performance
	- compensation (more common)
- termination of contraction
	- fundamental breaches:
		- repudiation: unable to fullfill with no reasonable justification
		- anticipatory breach: anticipate unable to fullfill contract which may or may not be a fundamental breach
- agency and authority
	- agency: engineer acts on behalf of client dealing with contractors.
- risk management
	- change circumstances: e.g. client change in scope
	- force majeure
	- conditional agreements: e.g. must obtain permits before engineering firm commits
	- limitation of liability: protect from excessive reliability
	- exemption clause: conditional agreements that predetermine external effectors
	- liquidated damages clause: specified compensation when breaches occur
	- indemnification clause: protection from negligence.

	- innocent: no intent

	- fraudulent: intentional deceit
