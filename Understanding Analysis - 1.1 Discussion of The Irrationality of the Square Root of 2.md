Abbott goes through milestones in the history of math to expound on its aesthetics:
- Hardy's ***A Mathematician's Apology*** defending pure math free of its application as an aesthetic discipline
- Leopold Kronecker: "The natural numbers are the work of God. All the rest is the work of mankind."
Abbot introduces some terms with loose definitions presumably to be expanded on later:
- ***Field:*** A set of elements where addition and multiplication are well-defined operations that are commutative, associative, distributive, contain the additive identity, additive inverse, multiplicative identity, and multiplicative inverse for all non-zero elements. More on this in Chapter 8.

The chapter also demonstrates <u>proof by contradiction</u> by proving the irrationality of $\sqrt{2}$ 


- Dirichlet introduced the formal definition of a function to formalize the Fourier series. It also let him create his wonky Dirichlet functions.
- [Reverse Triangle inequality.](Reverse%20Triangle%20inequality..md)
- If and only if forward and backward meaning
- <u>Proof by induction </u> :
For some set S that is a subset of  N
1)  $S$ contains 1 
2)  Whenever $S$ contains a natural number $n$, it also contains $n+1$ 
 Why is it a valid argumentative technique and its implications on natural numbers? for later. We'll return to Natural numbers after exploring $R$
