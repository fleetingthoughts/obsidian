## 🟢 DRILL BANK 1: Terminology Retrieval Solutions

- Q1.1 (A.J. Ayer): The problem of finding a logical or empirical proof that empirical generalizations derived from past experience will hold good in the future (ayer-langu... p. 5). Hempel considers it a problem because any formal deductive attempt treats a fact as a tautology, and any empirical attempt circularistically presupposes what it sets out to prove (ayer-langu... p. 5).
- Q1.2 (Godfrey-Smith): An inference from an observation to a hypothesis about an underlying structure, process, or cause that would logically explain that data (godfrey-sm... p. 5) (also called abduction or inference to the best explanation (godfrey-sm... p. 5)).
- Q1.3 (Plato): The class of goods that we would not choose to have for themselves, but strictly for the sake of their wages, rewards, or beneficial consequences (the_republ... pp. 1-2). Plato's exact examples are gymnastic exercise, medical treatment when sick, the practice of medicine, and moneymaking activities (the_republ... p. 1).
- Q1.4 (Plato): Tradesmen are static agents set up permanently inside the city's marketplace to buy and sell goods (the_republ... p. 14). Merchants are nomadic agents who wander across borders between different cities to facilitate international import and export (the_republ... p. 14).
- Q1.5 (Carl Hempel): Testing a hypothesis by seeking out, or waiting for, cases where the specified test conditions are realized spontaneously by nature (hempel-phi... p. 2). A scientist is forced to rely on it when available technological means make physical control or variation of the independent variables impossible (hempel-phi... p. 2) (e.g., studying stellar properties (hempel-phi... p. 4)).
- Q1.6 (Carl Hempel): Method of test: Experiencing or experimenting to evaluate a hypothesis that has been antecedently advanced (hempel-phi... p. 3). Method of discovery: A heuristic device where no specific hypothesis has been proposed yet; variables are systematically held constant or varied one-at-a-time to map out the exact mathematical form of functional dependence (hempel-phi... pp. 3-4).
- Q1.7 (A.J. Ayer): A true lie is genuine, unadulterated ignorance or deception held directly inside the soul about the nature of reality (the_republ... p. 26). A lie in speech is a mere downstream imitation, phantom, or spoken translation of that internal soul affection (the_republ... p. 26); it can be functionally useful as a preventative drug against enemies or mad friends (the_republ... p. 26).

## 📐 DRILL BANK 2: Logical Form Reconstruction Solutions

- Q2.1 (A.J. Ayer): Ayer shows that Hume's definitions fail on formal criteria:
    
    1. Defining cause as constant conjunction means asserting "C causes E" while denying that any C or E have ever been observed would be a self-contradiction, which it is not (ayer-langu... p. 13).
    2. Defining cause as a psychological association implies it is impossible for undiscovered causal laws to exist, which is false (ayer-langu... p. 13).
    
- Q2.2 (Godfrey-Smith): An object is _green_ if and only if it was first observed before 2010 A.D. and is _grue_, OR if it was not first observed before 2010 A.D. and is _bleen_ (godfrey-sm... p. 15).
- Q2.3 (Plato):
    
    1. Doing injustice is naturally good; suffering it is naturally bad (the_republ... p. 2).
    2. The harm of suffering it far exceeds the pleasure of doing it (the_republ... p. 2).
    3. _Synthesis_: Those lacking the power to escape the bad or safely choose the good form a defensive compact, establishing laws to name the middle ground "lawful and just" (the_republ... p. 3).
    
- Q2.4 (Plato):
    
    1. A city abandons its basic survival parameters to pursue unlimited luxury (the_republ... pp. 15-16).
    2. The home territory, initially sufficient for basic sustenance, becomes small and inadequate (the_republ... p. 16).
    3. _Consequence_: The city must violently seize a piece of its neighbors' land, overstepping the boundary of the necessary into unlimited monetary acquisition, triggering war (the_republ... p. 16).
    
- Q2.5 (Plato): The paradox states that a guardian must be both fiercely spirited (cruel to enemies) and gentle (to friends), which are psychologically antithetical traits, rendering a good guardian seemingly impossible (the_republ... p. 18). Resolution: A noble dog resolves this because it naturally greets familiars warmly and grows angry at strangers purely based on knowledge versus ignorance (the_republ... p. 19).
- Q2.6 (Carl Hempel): Brahe argued: If the Earth orbits the sun, stars must periodically shift angles (annual parallax) (hempel-phi... p. 5). His precise instruments detected zero parallax (hempel-phi... p. 6). He rejected Copernicanism (hempel-phi... p. 6). _The Flaw_: His structural equation relied on the false auxiliary hypothesis that the fixed stars were close enough to the Earth for their parallax movements to be visible without telescopes (hempel-phi... p. 6).
- Q2.7 (Carl Hempel): Duhem proved that a hypothesis $H$ can never be tested in complete isolation; it requires a cluster of auxiliary assumptions $A$ (hempel-phi... pp. 5, 10). Foucault's test did not isolate and crush Newton's corpuscular projectile concept itself; it only falsified the conjoined system of Newton's specific mechanical assumptions regarding how projectiles interact with mediums (hempel-phi... pp. 8-9).

## 🧠 DRILL BANK 3: Fallacy & Error Spotting Solutions

- Q3.1 (A.J. Ayer): The Grammatical Structure Illusion (ayer-langu... p. 7). This error falsely assumes that the grammatical framework of ordinary European languages is a trustworthy mirror of logical meaning (ayer-langu... p. 7), mistakenly parsing complex linguistic definitions as records of factual properties (ayer-langu... pp. 7, 16).
- Q3.2 (Godfrey-Smith): Content-Isolation Error (or Holism Blindspot) (godfrey-sm... p. 10). This treats confirmation as an absolute, automated formal relationship between two statements in a vacuum (godfrey-sm... p. 10), ignoring that an observation's relevance depends entirely on background parameters and generation procedures (godfrey-sm... pp. 10-11).
- Q3.3 (Godfrey-Smith): Reification of Artificial Kinds (godfrey-sm... p. 17). It mistakes a conventional, stipulated linguistic box or label for an objective "natural kind" anchored in a real, unified similarity in nature (godfrey-sm... p. 17).
- Q3.4 (Plato): The Theological Misattribution Fallacy (the_republ... p. 23). Because god is inherently good, god cannot cause harm, evil, or misery (the_republ... pp. 22-23). God must only be cited as the cause of the few things that are good, never the structural cause of evil or destruction (the_republ... p. 23).
- Q3.5 (Plato): The Plasticity / Allegorical Category Error (the_republ... p. 22). It overlooks that a young mind is structurally plastic and fundamentally incapable of distinguishing between a literal representation and a hidden allegorical meaning (the_republ... pp. 20, 22).
- Q3.6 (Plato): The Tool Automaticity Illusion (the_republ... p. 17). This error assumes a tool provides automatic skill on the day it is held (the_republ... p. 17). It ignores that complex arts (like war) require an innate baseline nature, absolute exemption from other tasks, and specialized training from childhood (the_republ... p. 17).
- Q3.7 (Carl Hempel): Ad Hoc Modification Error (hempel-phi... p. 11). This introduces a non-testable, uncalled-for qualification solely to insulate a failing hypothesis from adverse data (hempel-phi... p. 11), resulting in a complex, non-functional framework that yields no new test implications (hempel-phi... pp. 11-12).
