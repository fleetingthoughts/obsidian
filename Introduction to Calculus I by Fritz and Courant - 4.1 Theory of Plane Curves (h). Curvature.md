1) Definition of curvature as a derivative of the angle of inclination with respect to arc length
2) The parametric formula for curvature calculated using the definition of the angle of inclination with respect to the derivative
3) Derivation of the curvature in polar coordinates and in non-parametric form derived from 2)
4) A osculating curve as a relationship between two curves at a point to describe how similarly they behave at a point. For the non-parametric representation $y=f(x)$, two curves $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ osculate at a point $x=x_o$ if they satisfy the following:
	- They intersect at the point: $f(x_o)=g(x_o)$
	- They have the same tangent at that point: $f'(x_o)=g'(x)$
	- They have the same curvature at that point. In the non-parametric representation, this is satisfed by $f''(x_o)=g''(x_o)$
