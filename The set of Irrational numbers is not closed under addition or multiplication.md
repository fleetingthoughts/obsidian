While $\mathbb{Q}$ is closed under addition and multiplication (it is an ordered field), It can be shown that for any $r \in \mathbb{Q}$ and and irrational number $t \in \mathbb{I}$ that $r+t$ and $rt$ do not result in rational numbers. 

But at the same time we can find counterexamples to show that irrational numbers are not closed under addition. Consider the two irrational numbers $(1+\sqrt{2})$ and $(1-\sqrt{2})$ under multiplication for instance.
