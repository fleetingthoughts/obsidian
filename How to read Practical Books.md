Some key propositions include:
- The propositions are the rules or the principles and the evidence (deductive, inductive, illustrative, etc..)
- The arguments try to persuade you on the principles that generate these rules
- In general the books that give you the principles that generate the rules are superior to the books that are presentation of rules
- Persuasion and a propagandist language is necessary part of practical books that try to convince you. Learn to recognize them to avoid falling under its influence and remain objective
- The rules that are emphasizing and to be slightly modified are as follows:
	- Rule 4) "Determine the Questions the Author wants to answer" becomes "find out what the author wants you to do"
	- Rule 8: "Does the author answer his own problems" becomes "find out how he proposes you do what he wanted you to do"
