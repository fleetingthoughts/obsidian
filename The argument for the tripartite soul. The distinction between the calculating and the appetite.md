To argue for the tripartite soul, Socrates lays down logical groundwork by defining relations between elements in a way that distinguishes between relations between elements and particulars of that element. He examines our conventional use of language to distinguish between someone who's thirsty wanting a cold drink from someone who's thirsty wanting a hot drink, or knowledge itself and knowledge of particular area like medicine for example. What does his definition of relation mean for the two aforementioned examples and how is it used in conjunction with the principle of non-contradiction to distinguish the calculating part of the soul from the appetite?
