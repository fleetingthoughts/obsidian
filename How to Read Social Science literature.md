Adler compares social science more with the sciences than to fiction to illustrate his points. The main difficulty with reading social science literature is the "stipulation of usage" how does set up and define the terms or key ideas he will use to make his point. The difficulty arises because, in Adler's view, of the perceived ease in reading social sciences that makes use of very common terms like "culture" and "society". The ladenness of meaning of these terms in colloquial usages leads to ambiguity in understanding the way an author uses it. It becomes important to ensure you make no assumptions in how the author uses their own terms. In summary, the main difficulty is to address rule 5): coming to terms with the author.
