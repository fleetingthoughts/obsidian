## Piano & Musicianship Training Blueprint

Framework: 5 Hours per Week (1-Hour Daily Chunks)  
Core Text: _Harmony, Counterpoint, Partimento (HCP)_ by Job IJzerman  
Target Repertoire: Beethoven’s Sonata No. 8 in C minor, Op. 13 ("Pathétique")

---

## 1. Core Practice Guidelines from HCP (Chapters 1–4)

- Avoid Early Notation: Do not write down exercises immediately. This turns music into dry calculations. Notation is the final step after aural and physical mastery.
- Structural Reduction: Remove rhythmic decorations (diminutions) from complex exercises. Play or sing them first as plain, slow block chords to build analytical skills.
- Improvise Variations: Add your own melodic passing tones (diminutions) over plain exercises to develop creative fluency.
- Contrapuntal Splitting: Sing one voice aloud while your hands play the other voice(s) on the piano. Swap roles frequently.
- Rote Memorization: Memorize all core cadences and scale patterns. Practice transposing them into different keys and switching between major and minor modes purely by ear.

## 2. Skills & Benchmarks Matrix

## Chapter 1: Two-Part Scale Realization

- Core Skill: Internalize basic intervals (consonances vs. dissonances) over a moving bass. Apply minor scale rules smoothly (raised ➅/➆ ascend, natural ➅/➆ descend).
- Section 1.1 Goal: Accompany scales in parallel thirds/sixths, starting and ending on a stable octave.
- Section 1.2 Goal: Improvise a leaping upper voice using a 3–6 progression or Third Down Second Up schema.
- Section 1.3 & 1.4 Goal: Execute 7–6 suspensions (upper voice ties) and 2–3 suspensions (bass ties). Chain fluent 7–3 progressions over a Circle of Fifths bass.

## Chapter 2: Three-Part Scale Realization

- Core Skill: Treat chords ($^5_3$ and $^6_3$) as standalone horizontal combinations rather than vertical stacks. Guide middle voices to avoid parallel fifths and octaves.
- Section 2.1 Goal: Play a full Fauxbourdon scale up and down in three parts with sixths in the top voice, framed by $^8_3$ chords.
- Section 2.2 & 2.3 Goal: Play an ascending Monte sequence and transition it smoothly into a descending Stepwise Romanesca.
- Section 2.4 & 2.5 Goal: Realize a three-part Circle of Fifths ($^7_3$ chords) and master the Tied Bass schema using dissonant $^4_2$ chords resolving to $^6_3$.

## Chapter 3: The Three-Part Cadence

- Core Skill: Develop historical closing formulas across various keys to anchor structural phrases.
- Benchmark Goal: Memorize, transpose, and play 5 distinct historical cadences over jumping basses: Simple, Compound (7–6 or 2–3 suspensions), Double, Galant, and Neapolitan.

## Chapter 4: The Rule of the Octave (RO)

- Core Skill: Lock in the standard historical harmonization layout for every single step of an ascending or descending scale.
- Benchmark Goal: Play the complete Rule of the Octave by rote in major and minor keys. Sing the chord arpeggios aloud in close position while playing the scale progression underneath.

## 3. Daily 60-Minute Practice Schedule

```unset
⏱️ [00:00 - 00:10] Warm-up & Scale Technique
🎹 [00:10 - 00:30] Active Repertoire (Pathétique Focus)
🧠 [00:30 - 00:45] HCP Musicianship & Keyboard Improvisation
🔍 [00:45 - 00:55] Repertoire Analysis & Rote Review
📝 [00:55 - 01:00] Progression Log & Reflection
```

## Block 1: Warm-up & Technique (10 Mins)

- Action: Practice standard scales using the dynamic minor system (raise 6 and 7 going up, lower them going down). Run scales in parallel thirds and sixths to wake up finger spatial awareness.

## Block 2: Active Repertoire — Pathétique Focus (20 Mins)

- Action: Practice micro-targets (2 to 4 bars max).
    
    - _For Mvt 1:_ Isolate fast left-hand tremolos and rapid hand-crossing leaps slowly.
    - _For Mvt 2:_ Balance voice dynamics. Make the top melody sing while keeping the inner triplet accompaniment soft.
    

## Block 3: HCP Musicianship & Improvisation (15 Mins)

- Action: Work through your current textbook section using the author's multi-step method.
    
    - _Min 0–5:_ Play the bass line and sing the structural upper voice aloud.
    - _Min 5–10:_ Bring the full realization to the keyboard cleanly in a simple block-chord format.
    - _Min 10–15:_ Introduce small rhythm variations or passing notes (diminutions) over the framework.
    

## Block 4: Repertoire Analysis & Rote Review (10 Mins)

- Action: Scan your _Pathétique_ sheet music to find the exact schemata from the textbook (e.g., locating a Circle of Fifths or a Compound Cadence in the development section). Spend the remaining time playing Chapter 3 cadences or Chapter 4 Rule of the Octave entirely by rote to build muscle memory.

## Block 5: Log & Reflection (5 Mins)

- Action: Close the piano. Document specific bars where you felt physical tension, and list key signatures where your keyboard harmony felt slow so you know exactly what to target tomorrow.
