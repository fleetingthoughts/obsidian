1) Develop an exhaustive method to determine the arc length of a curve that satisfies the assumption of rectifiability:
	1) The limit exists.
	2) The value of the limit is independent of the way the method is constructed.
2) We provide geometric intuition to produce a Riemann sum and appeal to integral existence theorems outside the scope of the book to justify that the limit exists and independent of construction
3) Invariance of arclength with respect to a parameter $t$ to a valid change of parameter $\tau=\chi(t)$ (i.e. $\chi(t)$ is a monotonic function). We look at the arclength of two common changes of parameter:
	1) The univariate function
	2) Polar coordinates.
4) The integral properties deduce intuitive arclength properties like the additivity of arclength.
