Substitute $x=1$ to find $A=1$, and substitute $x=-1$ to find $B=1$. The decomposition is $\frac{1}{x-1} + \frac{1}{x+1}$.

* Strategy: Use u-substitution. Let $u = \cosh x$. The differential is $du = \sinh x \, dx$. The integral transforms to $\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln\vert{}u\vert{} + C$. Substituting back yields $\ln(\cosh x) + C$ (absolute value brackets are dropped since $\cosh x \geq 1$ for all real $x$).

## 📅 Day 7 Answer Key## Question 1

* Source: Section 4.1 (Curvature) (p. 15).
* Significance: Measures the structural geometry of the apex of a hanging loop to check its local bending density.
* Strategy: Use the explicit curvature equation: $\kappa = \frac{\vert{}y''\vert{}}{(1+(y')^2)^{3/2}}$. Compute derivatives for $y = \cosh x$: $y' = \sinh x$ and $y'' = \cosh x$. At baseline coordinate $x = 0$, evaluate values: $y'(0) = 0$ and $y''(0) = 1$. Substitute these values directly into the model to arrive at the solution: $\kappa = \frac{1}{(1+0)^{3/2}} =$ $1$.

## Question 2

* Source: Section 4.1 (Curvature - Parametric Form) (p. 15).
* Significance: Tracks how geometric bending constraints evolve dynamically along open hyperbolic path trajectories.
* Strategy: Differentiate components: $\dot{x} = \sinh t, \ddot{x} = \cosh t$ and $\dot{y} = \cosh t, \ddot{y} = \sinh t$. Deploy the parametric curvature equation: $\kappa = \frac{\vert{}\ddot{y}\dot{x} - \ddot{x}\dot{y}\vert{}}{((\dot{x})^2 + (\dot{y})^2)^{3/2}}$. The numerator evaluates to $\vert{}\sinh^2 t - \cosh^2 t\vert{} = \vert{}-1\vert{} = 1$. The denominator evaluates to $(\sinh^2 t + \cosh^2 t)^{3/2} = (\cosh(2t))^{3/2}$. The final curvature expression is $\frac{1}{(\cosh(2t))^{3/2}}$ (or $\text{sech}^{3/2}(2t)$).

## Question 3

* Source: Section 4.1 (Change of Coordinate Axes - Invariance) (p. 15).
* Significance: Provides algebraic proof that essential kinematic physical metrics remain unaffected by linear shifts or parameter updates in background coordinate frames.
* Strategy: Let a shift map be written as $x = \bar{x}\cosh\alpha + \bar{y}\sinh\alpha$ and $y = \bar{x}\sinh\alpha + \bar{y}\cosh\alpha$. Differentiating creates the structural matrix pairing: $\dot{x} = \dot{\bar{x}}\cosh\alpha + \dot{\bar{y}}\sinh\alpha$ and $\dot{y} = \dot{\bar{x}}\sinh\alpha + \dot{\bar{y}}\cosh\alpha$. Expand the difference of squares:
$$\dot{x}^2 - \dot{y}^2 = (\dot{\bar{x}}\cosh\alpha + \dot{\bar{y}}\sinh\alpha)^2 - (\dot{\bar{x}}\sinh\alpha + \dot{\bar{y}}\cosh\alpha)^2$$ 
$$= \dot{\bar{x}}^2(\cosh^2\alpha - \sinh^2\alpha) - \dot{\bar{y}}^2(\cosh^2\alpha - \sinh^2\alpha) + 2\dot{\bar{x}}\dot{\bar{y}}(\cosh\alpha\sinh\alpha - \sinh\alpha\cosh\alpha)$$ 
Since $\cosh^2\alpha - \sinh^2\alpha = 1$ and the cross-terms cancel to zero, this leaves $\dot{\bar{x}}^2 - \dot{\bar{y}}^2$, proving full invariant protection under spatial transformations.
