1) Definition of the moment of a curve center of mass relative to the $x$-axis in the discrete case
2) Apply the definition of the mass to a curve of uniform "mass per length density". This is a typical physics "infinitesimal" argument that will be used to set up an infinite sum in the limiting case
3) Passage of the sum through an integral to equate the limiting case to an integral. Note that sum is a product of two functions $y(x_1)\sqrt{1+y'(x_2)}$ where $x_1$ and $x_2$ do not have to equal. The product does not satisfy the definition of the Riemann sum, but we appeal to Bliss's theorem beyond the scope of this book that the infinite sum of a product of two functions is equal to the integral.
4) Derive the formula for the moment of a curve, $T$, and the center of mass, $\eta$, of a curve relative to the $x$-axis.
